Common Questions

Can hyphema heal on its own?
Mild hyphemas may resolve in 5-10 days with proper rest and medical supervision, but medical attention is essential to monitor eye pressure.
How long does recovery take?
Recovery time varies by severity: mild (5-7 days), moderate (1-2 weeks), severe (2-4 weeks or longer with treatment).
Can I exercise with hyphema?
No. Avoid any strenuous activity, heavy lifting, or bending over as these can increase eye pressure and cause re-bleeding.
When can I return to work/school?
Only after your ophthalmologist approves. This depends on severity and resolution of bleeding.

Emergency Situations

Seek immediate medical help if you experience:

  • Sudden vision loss
  • Severe eye pain
  • Large amount of blood in eye
  • Nausea or vomiting with eye pain
  • Flashes of light or floaters
